"Quackery, which is frequently associated with health fraud, involves the promotion of deceptive or uninformed medical practices. A quack is an individual who falsely claims to possess medical expertise or qualifications they lack, essentially a charlatan or someone akin to a snake oil salesman. The term 'quack' is a shortened version of the old term 'quacksalver,' derived from the Dutch word 'kwakzalver,' meaning a person who peddles ointments. In medieval times, 'quack' referred to 'shouting,' as these quacksalvers used to attract attention by loudly advertising their products at markets. Typical features of general quackery encompass the use of dubious diagnoses based on unreliable tests and the promotion of unproven or debunked treatments, particularly for severe ailments like cancer. Quackery is often labeled as 'health fraud,' with a key characteristic being its aggressive promotion."

Quackery refers to the promotion or selling of medical practices, products, or services that are not scientifically proven, ineffective, or fraudulent. This definition encompasses a wide range of actions, including the promotion of questionable ideas, products, or services, even if the promoters genuinely believe in their effectiveness. The key distinction in this definition is that the term "fraud" is reserved for situations where there is deliberate deception involved, meaning that the promoters knowingly mislead others. One of the major ethical issues associated with quackery is that it may lead people to choose ineffective or unproven treatments over evidence-based medical care. People who fall for quackery may forego legitimate medical treatments that could actually help them, in favor of treatments or products offered by individuals referred to as "quacks." This can be harmful to patients' health and well-being. It's important for individuals to critically evaluate health claims and treatments and rely on reputable sources and evidence-based medicine to make informed decisions about their healthcare. Additionally, regulatory authorities and medical professionals play a significant role in identifying and combating quackery to protect public health.
